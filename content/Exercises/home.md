### Ever found yourself doing the same proof over and over again?
You know the drill: you are studying something, and to really understand it, you sit down and work through a few exercises or proofs.
Then, weeks or months later, you need that exact result again -- and of course, you have completely forgotten how you did it!
So you redo all the steps from scratch. Again. For the hundredth time...


Happens to me *all* the time!
I have probably rederived the Chernoff bound more times than I'd like to admit -- starting from Markov’s inequality and going through all the usual steps, until that exponential tail finally shows up.
<!--via the moment-generating function trick.-->
Every time I need it, I end up repeating the same reasoning from scratch.

So I have decided to use this page to write down those little proofs and exercises I work out while studying (the ones that are a pain to redo but super useful to have on hand).

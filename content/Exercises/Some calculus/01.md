### $\sqrt{(n-1) \log{n}} = (1+o(1))\sqrt{n\log{n}}$

We first start with the following inequality
$$
\sqrt{(n-1)\log{n}} = \sqrt{n\log{n} - \log{n}} = \sqrt{n\log{n} \cdot \left( 1+\frac{1}{n}\right)} = \sqrt{n\log{n}} \cdot \sqrt{ 1+\frac{1}{n}} 
$$

Let $x = 1/n$, and for large values of $n \to \infty$ we have that $x \to 0^+$.
For $x = 0$, the function $f(x) = \sqrt{1-x}$ has Taylor expansion
$$
\begin{aligned}
\sqrt{1-x}
&= f(0) + f'(0)(x-0) + o(x)\\
&= 1 -\frac{x}{2} + o(x)
\end{aligned}
$$


> [!remind] 
> Remind
> $$
> \frac{d}{dx} \sqrt{1-x} = \frac{1}{2 \sqrt{1-x}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} (1-x) = - \frac{1}{2\sqrt{1-x}}
> $$


Since $x = 1/n$, we have
$$
\sqrt{1-\frac{1}{n}} = 1- \frac{1}{2n} + o\left( \frac{1}{n}\right)
$$
and thus (for $n \to \infty$)
$$
\sqrt{(n-1)\log{n}} = \sqrt{n\log{n}} \cdot \left( 1- \frac{1}{2n} + o\left( \frac{1}{n}\right) \right) = (1 + o(1)) \sqrt{n \log{n}}
$$
since $-1/2n + o(1/n) = o(1/n)$.

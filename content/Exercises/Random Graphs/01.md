## Erdős-Rényi Random Graphs

We denote by $G(n,p)$ the Erdős-Rényi random graph model.
In this model, we have a set of $n$ vertices, and each pair of vertices is connected by an edge with probability $p$, *independently* of all other pairs.
It is simple to see that the number of edges in a graph $G \sim G(n,p)$ follows a [binomial distribution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Binomial_distribution) with parameters $\binom{n}{2}$ and $p$, i.e., $E[\text{\# edges}] = \binom{n}{2} p$.

### Properties of Erdős-Rényi Random Graphs

> [!theorem]
> Let $G \sim G(n,1/2)$. Then, for every $v \in V(G)$, we have $\delta_v = \frac{n-1}{2} \pm \sqrt{(n-1)\ln{n}}$ with high probability, where $\delta_v$ is the degree of vertex $v$.

> [!help] Proof
> The degree $\delta_v$ of a vertex $v \in V(G)$ follows a binomial distribution over the all $n-1$ edges incident to $v$. Therefore
> $$
> \mathbb{E}\delta_v = \frac{n-1}{2}.
> $$
> By applying the [[Exercises/Probability/01.md#Chernoff's inequality: Additive form|Chernoff's inequality: Additive form]], we have
> $$
> \Pr\left\lbrace \left\vert \delta_v - \mathbb{E}\delta_v \right\vert \geq t \right\rbrace =
> \Pr\left\lbrace \delta_v \geq \mathbb{E}\delta_v + t \right\rbrace + \Pr\left\lbrace \delta_v \leq \mathbb{E}\delta_v - t \right\rbrace
> \leq 2\exp\left(-\frac{2t^2}{n-1}\right).
> $$
> 
> Finally, choosing $t = \sqrt{(n-1)\ln{n}}$, we have
> $$
> \Pr\left\lbrace \left\vert \delta_v - \mathbb{E}\delta_v \right\vert \geq \sqrt{(n-1)\ln{n}} \right\rbrace \leq 2\exp\left(-2\ln{n}\right) = 2n^{-2}.
> $$
> Thus, with high probability, we have $\delta_v = \frac{n-1}{2} \pm \sqrt{(n-1)\ln{n}}$.

 
> [!question] Corollary
> As a consequence of [[Exercises/Some Calculations/01#$ sqrt{(n-1) log{n}} = (1+o(1)) sqrt{n log{n}}$|this inequality]], we have that the average degree of every vertex $v$ in a random graph $G \sim G(n,1/2)$ is $\frac{n-1}{2} \pm (1+o(1))\sqrt{n\log{n}}$ with high probability.
